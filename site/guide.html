Tallywell User Guide

A complete guide for therapists new to the app.

1. What is Tallywell?

Tallywell is a free app that helps therapists keep track of their income and sessions. If you see clients through multiple channels — an insurance platform like Alma or Headway, private-pay clients, and/or an employer — Tallywell gives you one clear picture of what you've earned, what each payer still owes you, and how your caseload looks across all your sources.

What Tallywell is not

  • Not an EHR. It does not store clinical notes, diagnoses, treatment plans, or any information beyond session date, client initials, service code, and dollar amounts.
  • Not a billing system. It does not submit claims to insurance. Your payer platforms handle that.
  • Not tax software. It helps you organize income for your accountant, but is not a substitute for professional tax advice.

What makes it different

Your data never leaves your computer. There is no server, no account, no cloud sync. Everything is stored locally, encrypted with your passphrase. No one else — not even the developer — can access your data.

Free, always. No subscription, no trial period, no premium tier. Tallywell is open-source (MIT license).

2. Installing

macOS

  1. Go to the download page and click macOS (DMG).
  2. When the download finishes, open Tallywell.dmg (look in your Downloads folder or your browser's download bar).
  3. A window opens showing the Tallywell icon and your Applications folder. Drag Tallywell into the Applications folder.
  4. Eject the disk image: drag the Tallywell disk from your Desktop to the Trash (the Trash icon turns into an Eject icon while dragging), or right-click it and choose Eject.
  5. Open your Applications folder and double-click Tallywell.
First-launch warning from macOS: You may see a message saying "Tallywell cannot be opened because it is from an unidentified developer." This happens because Tallywell is not yet signed with an Apple certificate. It is safe to open. To do so: right-click (or Control-click) the Tallywell icon in your Applications folder, choose Open, then click Open again in the dialog. You only need to do this once.

Windows

  1. Click Windows on the download page.
  2. When the download finishes, double-click tallywell-windows-amd64.exe.
  3. Windows SmartScreen may show a warning. Click More info then Run anyway.
  4. Tallywell opens in your default browser.

Linux

  1. Click Linux on the download page.
  2. Open a Terminal, navigate to where the file downloaded, and run:
    chmod +x tallywell-linux-amd64 && ./tallywell-linux-amd64
  3. Tallywell opens in your default browser.
Tallywell runs as a small local server on your computer and opens in your browser. The browser is just the window — no data is sent to the internet.

3. First launch: creating your passphrase

The very first time you open Tallywell, you'll see the setup screen.

Your passphrase is the key that encrypts all your data. Tallywell has no "Forgot my passphrase" option — if you lose it, your data cannot be recovered. Please take a moment here:

  • Choose a phrase you'll remember, but that others won't easily guess. A few random words strung together work well (e.g. purple-lamp-tuesday-river).
  • Write it down in a trusted, secure place: a password manager, a printed sheet kept somewhere safe, or a note in your personal vault.
  • Use at least 12 characters.

Type your passphrase in the first field, type it again to confirm, then click Set passphrase. You'll land on the dashboard.

4. Unlocking daily

Every time you open Tallywell after the initial setup, you'll see the unlock screen instead of setup.

Enter your passphrase and click Unlock. If you type the wrong passphrase, you'll see "Incorrect passphrase." — check that Caps Lock is off and try again.

Auto-lock

For security, Tallywell automatically locks after 15 minutes of inactivity, even if the browser tab stays open. When that happens, you'll be taken back to the unlock screen. This protects client data if you step away from your computer.

You can also lock the app at any time by clicking the Lock button in the navigation bar at the top of any page.

Forgotten passphrase: There is no recovery option — this is by design. The passphrase is the encryption key; without it the data cannot be decrypted. Keeping a copy in a password manager or other safe location is strongly recommended.

5. Setting up your account

Before you can log sessions, tell Tallywell about the practices you work in and the payers who pay you. Click Settings in the top navigation bar.

Adding a practice

A "practice" represents a context you work in. Most therapists have one practice (their own private practice) or two (their own practice plus an employer). The distinction matters at tax time: Tallywell keeps W-2 employer income separate from self-employment income.

  1. Under Practices, type the name of your practice (e.g. "My Practice" or your actual practice name).
  2. Choose the type: My own practice if you're self-employed here, or An employer if this is a W-2 job.
  3. Click Add practice.

If you have both your own practice and an employer, add both separately.

Adding payers

A "payer" is anyone who pays you for sessions: an insurance platform, private-pay clients, or an employer. Add one payer for each distinct source of income.

  1. Under Payers, type the name of the payer (e.g. "Alma", "Headway", "Private Pay").
  2. Choose which practice this payer is associated with.
  3. Choose the type:
    • Insurance platform — Alma, Headway, Grow Therapy, Rula, etc.
    • Private pay — clients who pay you directly.
    • Employer — a group practice, agency, or other W-2 employer.
  4. Leave the Importer field blank for now (used for CSV import, which is coming soon).
  5. Click Add payer.
Example setup for a therapist with two platforms and private pay: add "Alma" (Insurance platform), "Headway" (Insurance platform), and "Private Pay" (Private pay) — three payers total.

6. Setting rates

Click Rates in the navigation bar. Rates tell Tallywell how much each payer pays per session type, so expected amounts fill in automatically when you log a session.

  1. Choose a payer from the dropdown.
  2. Enter a service code (e.g. 90837). If you always charge the same rate for every session type with this payer, leave it blank to set a default rate.
  3. Enter the amount (e.g. $150 or 150).
  4. Click Add.

Repeat for each payer. If a payer pays different amounts for different session lengths, add a separate rate for each service code.

7. Logging sessions

Click Sessions in the navigation bar. Use the form at the top to log each session as it happens — or in advance for scheduled appointments.

The session form fields

  • Date — the date the session took place (or is scheduled to take place).
  • Client — the client's initials or an ID you use to identify them (e.g. "AB" or "C-042"). Never enter a full name.
  • Payer — which payer bills this session.
  • Service — the CPT code (e.g. 90837). If you've set a rate for this payer and service, the Expected amount fills in automatically.
  • Status — choose one:
    • Completed — the session happened.
    • Scheduled — the session is upcoming.
    • No-show — the client didn't attend.
    • Cancelled — the session was cancelled.
  • Expected — what you expect to be paid. Auto-filled from your rate; you can override it if needed.
  • Paid — check this box when the payer has actually sent payment for this session.

Click Add to save. The session appears in the list below the form.

Workflow tip: Don't wait until a session is paid before logging it. Log it on the day it happens with Status: Completed, then check the Paid box when the money arrives — often 2–6 weeks later for insurance platforms. The dashboard shows what's still outstanding so you always know what you're waiting on.

Reading the sessions list

Completed sessions that haven't been marked paid are highlighted so they stand out. The list shows date, client, payer, service, status, expected amount, amount paid, and date paid. Scroll down to see older sessions.

8. Reading the dashboard

Click Home in the navigation bar (or just open the app) to see your dashboard. It gives a real-time summary of your income across all payers.

The metric cards

  • Earned — total expected income from all completed sessions. This is what you've worked for.
  • Still owed to you — earned income that hasn't been paid yet. The number you're waiting on.
  • Sessions — total number of sessions logged.
  • Paid so far — income you've actually received.

Income by source

A table breaking down each payer: how many sessions, how much earned, and how much is still outstanding. This helps you quickly see if a particular platform is slow to pay.

Employer vs own practice

If you have both an employer and your own practice, this section separates the income. This is the number you'll hand to your accountant at tax time: employer income is W-2, own-practice income is self-employment.

9. Importing from payer platforms (coming soon)

The Import page will let you upload CSV export files from platforms like Alma, Headway, and SimplePractice. Tallywell will automatically match the payouts to the sessions you've logged and mark sessions as paid.

Until this feature is available, mark sessions as paid by hand:

  1. When a platform pays you, check your platform's payment summary to see which sessions are included.
  2. Go to Sessions and find those sessions in the list. Re-add them with the Paid checkbox checked, or note the date paid.

Once CSV import is available, this will be much faster — you'll just upload the file and let Tallywell do the matching.

10. Exporting for your accountant

Click Export in the top navigation bar at any time to download a spreadsheet of all your data.

The spreadsheet opens in Numbers (Mac), Microsoft Excel, or Google Sheets. It includes:

  • A complete list of all sessions with amounts and payment status.
  • Income totals by payer and by month.
  • A summary separating employer (W-2) income from self-employment income — useful for your accountant at tax time.
  • Any sessions that couldn't be matched (for review).

There is no need to coordinate with Tallywell when sharing this file — just email the spreadsheet to your accountant as you would any other attachment.

Export as often as you like. Each export reflects your current data; nothing is deleted or changed by exporting.

11. Locking and security

Tallywell protects your data with a passphrase that encrypts everything stored on disk. Even if someone copies your data file, they cannot read it without your passphrase.

Locking manually

Click the Lock button in the top navigation bar whenever you step away from your computer. The app immediately returns to the unlock screen and clears your data from memory.

Auto-lock

The app locks automatically after 15 minutes of inactivity, even if the browser tab is open. This meets HIPAA's automatic logoff requirement. When you return, enter your passphrase to continue where you left off.

Tips for keeping data secure

  • Enable FileVault on your Mac (System Settings → Privacy & Security → FileVault). This encrypts your entire drive and adds another layer of protection if your computer is stolen.
  • Use a strong passphrase and store it in a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, Apple's built-in Keychain, etc.).
  • Don't share your passphrase. Anyone with it can access all your data.
  • Lock when away. Get in the habit of clicking Lock before stepping out of your office, even briefly.
Tallywell is designed for HIPAA-conscious use. Whether your specific use meets HIPAA requirements depends on your full set of practices — Tallywell is a tool, not a guarantee. Consult your compliance officer or attorney if you have questions.

12. Quitting Tallywell

Click Quit in the top navigation bar (next to the Lock button). This shuts down the Tallywell server cleanly and stops the app.

All your data is saved automatically every time you make a change. There is no Save button — if you see data on screen, it's already saved.

Don't just close the browser tab. Closing the tab hides the window but leaves the Tallywell server running in the background. Always use the Quit button to fully stop the app.

13. Backing up your data

Your Tallywell data lives in a single encrypted file on your computer. Backing it up is just copying that file somewhere safe.

Where the data file is

  • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/Tallywell/tracker.db.enc
  • Windows: %APPDATA%\Tallywell\tracker.db.enc
  • Linux: ~/.local/share/Tallywell/tracker.db.enc

How to back up (macOS)

  1. In Finder, open the menu bar and choose Go → Go to Folder.
  2. Type ~/Library/Application Support/Tallywell/ and press Return.
  3. Copy tracker.db.enc to an external drive, USB stick, or iCloud Drive. The file is already encrypted with your passphrase, so it's safe to store in the cloud.

Restoring from backup

  1. Install Tallywell on the new computer.
  2. On first launch, create a passphrase — use the exact same passphrase as the original installation.
  3. Copy your backup tracker.db.enc file to the data folder (same path as above).
  4. Relaunch Tallywell and unlock with your passphrase. All your data will be there.
Back up after any major data entry session — after logging a week's sessions, for example. Keeping a dated copy in iCloud Drive or on an external drive gives you a recovery point if your computer is lost or damaged.

14. Getting help

If something isn't working as expected, you can report it on GitHub Issues. You'll need a free GitHub account to submit a new report.

When reporting a problem, it helps to include:

  • What you were trying to do.
  • What happened instead.
  • Your operating system (macOS version, Windows version, etc.).
  • The Tallywell version (visible in the GitHub release notes).

You can also view the source code on GitHub. Contributions — especially CSV importers for additional payer platforms — are very welcome.

Tallywell is not an EHR, and not tax or legal advice. It is designed for HIPAA-conscious, local-first use; security depends on your own device and practices.
