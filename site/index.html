Tallywell

One clear picture of your sessions and income.

For therapists juggling several payers — insurance platforms, private pay, and an employer. See what you've earned, what's still owed, and how your caseload looks, without logging into four portals.

Local-first. No servers, no accounts. Your client data never leaves your computer, encrypted on disk. Opens in its own native window — no browser required. Supports light and dark mode.

What it does

  • Opens in its own native window — no browser tab to lose track of.
  • Log sessions by hand, or import a payer's CSV export.
  • Automatically reconciles sessions against payouts that arrive weeks later.
  • Shows income by source and month, and what's still outstanding.
  • Keeps employer (W-2) income separate from your own practice for taxes.
  • Exports a clean spreadsheet for you or your accountant anytime.
  • Adapts to your system's light or dark mode automatically.
  • Need a fresh start? A reset option wipes all data — with multiple warnings before doing so. Includes step-by-step uninstall instructions per platform.

Installing on macOS

  1. Open Tallywell.dmg.
  2. Drag Tallywell into the Applications folder shown in the window.
  3. Eject the disk image, then open Tallywell from Applications or Launchpad.

On first launch macOS may warn that the app is from an unidentified developer (it isn't notarized yet). To allow it: right-click the app in Finder, choose Open, then confirm. After that it opens normally.

Installing on Windows

  1. Download Tallywell-Setup.exe and double-click it.
  2. The installer adds Tallywell to your Start Menu — no administrator password required.
  3. Open Tallywell from the Start Menu or click Launch at the end of the installer.

Running on Linux

  1. Download Tallywell.AppImage.
  2. Open a Terminal, navigate to your Downloads folder, and run:
    chmod +x Tallywell.AppImage && ./Tallywell.AppImage
  3. Tallywell opens in its own window.

The AppImage requires libwebkit2gtk-4.1 (WebKitGTK). On Ubuntu 24.04+/Debian: sudo apt install libwebkit2gtk-4.1-0. On Arch: webkit2gtk-4.1. On Fedora: webkit2gtk4.1. Most GTK-based desktops already have it installed.
